# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

BubbleFish Nexus is an open-source project that welcomes contributors regardless of experience level, background, or identity. We are committed to providing a harassment-free experience for everyone.

This code of conduct applies to all project spaces: the GitHub repository, issue tracker, pull requests, discussions, and any other channel where project participants interact.

---

## Standards

**Expected behavior:**

- Be respectful and constructive in all interactions
- Provide and accept feedback graciously
- Focus on what is best for the project and its users
- Assume good intent in others' contributions and questions
- Respect differing viewpoints and experiences

**Unacceptable behavior:**

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory comments
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Sustained disruption of discussions or contribution processes
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all interactions within BubbleFish Nexus project spaces. It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces (for example, using an official project email address or acting as an appointed representative at an event).

---

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers at:

**conduct@bubblefish.sh**

All reports will be reviewed and investigated. The project team is obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter. Responses may include:

1. **Correction** — A private written warning with clarity about the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate.
2. **Warning** — A public or private warning with consequences for continued behavior.
3. **Temporary ban** — Temporary removal from project interaction spaces.
4. **Permanent ban** — Permanent removal from all project interaction spaces.

The project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this code of conduct.
