## When you need a commercial license

You need a commercial license if you want to do any of the following:

- **Offer Nexus as a service** to third parties without releasing your complete source code under AGPL-3.0
- **Embed Nexus** in a proprietary product that you distribute to customers
- **Modify Nexus** and keep those modifications private
- **Use Nexus** in an environment where your organization's policy prohibits AGPL-licensed software

If you run Nexus internally for your own team — even in production, even at scale — the AGPL-3.0 license covers that at no cost. The commercial license is only required when AGPL obligations would apply and you cannot or do not wish to comply with them.

## What the commercial license grants

The commercial license is a separate agreement between you (the Licensee) and BubbleFish Technologies, Inc. (the Licensor). It grants:

1. **Freedom from AGPL obligations.** You may use, modify, and distribute Nexus without the source-disclosure and network-use requirements of AGPL-3.0.

2. **Modification rights.** You may create derivative works and keep them proprietary.

3. **Redistribution rights.** You may include Nexus in products you sell or distribute to your customers.

4. **SaaS rights.** You may offer Nexus (or services built on it) to third parties over a network without triggering AGPL Section 13.

5. **Support and maintenance.** Commercial licenses include access to priority support channels and guaranteed response times, as specified in your Order Form.

## What the commercial license does not change

- The AGPL-3.0 version of Nexus remains available to everyone, forever. A commercial license does not remove or restrict the public AGPL release.
- Contributors retain copyright on their contributions (per the CLA, they grant BubbleFish Technologies, Inc. a license to sublicense under commercial terms).
- If your commercial license expires or is terminated, you retain the right to use Nexus under AGPL-3.0. You lose only the commercial-specific permissions.

## FAQ

**Can I evaluate Nexus before buying a commercial license?**
Yes. Use the AGPL-3.0 version for evaluation, development, and internal testing at no cost and with no time limit. You only need a commercial license when you ship to production in a context where AGPL obligations would apply.

**Does the commercial license cover future versions?**
The license covers the version specified in your Order Form plus all minor and patch releases in the same major version series (e.g., a license for 0.1.x covers 0.1.0 through 0.1.99). Major version upgrades require a renewal or upgrade agreement.

**What if I contribute code back to Nexus?**
Contributions to the AGPL-3.0 repository are governed by the [Contributor License Agreement](CLA.md). Your contributions remain available under AGPL-3.0 to everyone, and the CLA grants BubbleFish Technologies, Inc. the right to include them in commercially-licensed distributions.

**Is there a free tier for startups or open-source projects?**
Contact us. We evaluate requests on a case-by-case basis.
